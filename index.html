Biography

About Me

I am a M.Tech (CSE) Research Scholar at Madanapalle Institute of Technology & Science (MITS), Andhra Pradesh, India, working at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence, Explainable AI, and domain-specific intelligent systems. My research focuses on developing transparent, trustworthy, and practically deployable AI models across high-stakes domains including healthcare, finance, and urban systems.

With 8 accepted international conference papers and 6 accepted book chapters—all currently in press—my work spans explainable machine learning, financial risk forecasting, federated learning architectures, quantum-classical hybrid models, and low-resource natural language processing. Each contribution is oriented toward bridging the gap between algorithmic innovation and interpretable, real-world deployment.

My research philosophy is grounded in scientific rigor and applied relevance: I seek to build AI systems that are not only accurate, but auditable, equitable, and actionable. I am actively pursuing doctoral opportunities where I can deepen my contributions to AI safety, explainability, and intelligent decision-making under uncertainty.

Beyond independent research, I engage regularly in academic knowledge exchange through seminars, peer collaboration, and mentoring junior students on applied machine learning projects.

Applied Research

Research Projects

Healthcare AI

AI-Based Medical Imaging & Diagnosis

  • Deployed CNN architectures (ResNet, EfficientNet) for multi-class retinal and pulmonary disease classification
  • Integrated Grad-CAM and SHAP-based saliency maps for clinical interpretability
  • Achieved robust performance on small annotated datasets via transfer learning and augmentation pipelines
  • Designed for deployment in low-resource diagnostic settings with minimal computational overhead
Financial Intelligence

AI-Driven Investment Risk Prediction Framework

  • Built ensemble models (XGBoost, LightGBM, Random Forest) for multi-horizon investment risk forecasting
  • Incorporated macroeconomic indicators, sentiment analysis, and technical signals as feature inputs
  • Applied SHAP attribution for per-prediction interpretability aligned with regulatory transparency requirements
  • Evaluated against classical risk metrics including VaR, CVaR, and drawdown-adjusted return ratios
Insurance Analytics

Smart Risk Scoring for Insurance Analytics

  • Designed an end-to-end ML pipeline for automated policyholder risk stratification and premium calibration
  • Applied gradient boosting and logistic regression with calibrated probability outputs
  • Integrated LIME and SHAP for per-applicant risk factor explanations suitable for auditors and underwriters
  • Benchmarked against actuarial baseline models demonstrating improved accuracy and interpretability
